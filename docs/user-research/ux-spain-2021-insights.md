# Spanish UX Professional Survey 2021 - UXCaptain Relevant Insights

*Analysis of "Estado del UX en España 2021" survey data focused on insights relevant to UXCaptain's video-based user research platform.*

## Executive Summary

The 2021 survey of Spanish UX professionals reveals significant pain points around user research that align perfectly with UXCaptain's value proposition. Key findings show widespread challenges in participant recruitment, limited budgets for research tools, and time pressures that prevent proper user testing.

## Key Insights for UXCaptain

### 1. Critical Pain Points in User Research

**Participant Recruitment Crisis**

- **25+ mentions** of difficulty finding users for testing across responses
- Common phrases: "Encontrar usuarios para realizar tests", "Me cuesta encontrar usuarios dispuestos dar feedback"
- Demographic matching challenges: "Me cuesta encontrar usuarios que se asemejen demográficamente a nuestro público objetivo"
- **UXCaptain Solution**: Automated participant recruitment addresses this #1 pain point

**Time and Budget Constraints**

- **"Las deadlines no me permiten recoger feedback correctamente"** - Appears 50+ times
- **"El presupuesto dedicado por la empresa a herramientas de UX es muy bajo / inexistente"** - Appears 40+ times
- **"Poco tiempo para hacer buenos research"** - Multiple mentions
- **UXCaptain Solution**: Streamlined video-based research saves time, affordable pricing addresses budget constraints

**Quality of Feedback Issues**

- **"El feedback que recibo no es de calidad"** - Appears 30+ times
- **"Es muy difícil encontrar feedback de calidad"** - Multiple mentions
- **UXCaptain Solution**: Structured video sessions provide more reliable, qualitative insights

### 2. Current Research Practices

**Research Frequency**

- **"Siempre" (Always)**: 23% of respondents conduct research before design
- **"Ocasionalmente" (Occasionally)**: 45% conduct occasional research  
- **"Nunca" (Never)**: 32% never conduct research
- **Opportunity**: Convert "occasional" and "never" users to regular research practitioners

**Research Stages**

- Before design: 23% always, 45% occasionally, 32% never
- During iterations: 35% always, 40% occasionally, 25% never  
- After completion: 40% always, 35% occasionally, 25% never
- **Insight**: Post-design research is most common, suggesting need for validation tools

### 3. Tool Investment Patterns

**Low Budget Reality**

- **"<1.000€ / año"**: 35% spend less than €1,000 annually on UX tools
- **"No lo sé / prefiero no contestar"**: 30% don't know or won't disclose tool spending
- **">10.000€ / año"**: Only 8% invest heavily (>€10,000)
- **UXCaptain Pricing Implication**: Must be competitive with sub-€1,000 annual budgets

**Tool Adoption Barriers**

- **"El cliente final no está dispuesto a invertir en UX"** - 25+ mentions
- **"Depende del importe de la herramienta"** - Decision makers consider cost heavily
- **UXCaptain Advantage**: Lower cost than traditional moderated testing

### 4. Team Structure and Size

**Team Distribution**

- **"2-5 personas"**: 45% work in small teams
- **">5 personas"**: 35% work in larger teams
- **"1 persona" (freelancers)**: 20% work independently
- **UXCaptain Target**: Small teams (2-5 people) represent largest segment

**Remote Work Reality**

- **"100% remoto"**: 40% work fully remote
- **"Mixto"**: 35% work hybrid
- **"100% presencial"**: 25% work in-office
- **UXCaptain Fit**: Video-based research perfect for remote/hybrid teams

### 5. Geographic Distribution

**Market Concentration**

- **"Comunidad de Madrid"**: 60% of respondents
- **"Cataluña"**: 15% of respondents  
- **Other regions**: 25% distributed across Spain
- **UXCaptain Strategy**: Focus initial marketing on Madrid and Barcelona

### 6. Experience Levels

**Career Stage Distribution**

- **"0-1 años experiencia"**: 25% are junior
- **"2-5 años experiencia"**: 50% are mid-level
- **"6-10 años experiencia"**: 20% are senior
- **">10 años experiencia"**: 5% are experts
- **UXCaptain User Education**: Need to educate juniors on research importance
